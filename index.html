The Global Water Crisis

Water scarcity affects more than 40% of the global population, and this number is projected to rise.

Why Water Conservation Matters

Only 2.5% of Earth's water is freshwater, and less than 1% is accessible for human use. With growing populations and climate change, water scarcity is becoming one of the most critical challenges of our time.

AquaGuard was founded in 2015 to address the global water crisis through education, innovation, and community action. We work with local communities, governments, and businesses to implement sustainable water management solutions.

Our approach combines traditional conservation methods with cutting-edge technology to protect watersheds, improve water quality, and promote efficient water use in agriculture, industry, and households.

Essential Water Facts

Understanding the facts is the first step toward making a difference in water conservation.

Industrial Water Use

Industry accounts for approximately 22% of global water consumption. In developed countries, this can be as high as 59%.

Household Water Consumption

The average American family uses more than 300 gallons of water per day at home. Approximately 70% of this is used indoors.

Virtual Water

Producing a single cup of coffee requires about 37 gallons of water (mostly for growing the coffee beans). This is known as "virtual water."

Climate Change Impact

Climate change is altering precipitation patterns, causing more frequent and severe droughts and floods, directly impacting water availability.

Water Conservation Tips

Small changes in daily habits can make a big difference in water conservation.

Fix Leaks

A single dripping faucet can waste more than 3,000 gallons of water per year. Regularly check and fix leaks in your home.

Shorter Showers

Reducing your shower time by just 2 minutes can save up to 1,750 gallons of water per person annually.

Efficient Toilets

Toilets account for nearly 30% of indoor water use. Consider installing low-flow or dual-flush toilets.

Full Loads Only

Run your dishwasher and washing machine only when they're full. This can save up to 1,000 gallons of water per month.

Water-Smart Landscaping

Choose native, drought-resistant plants for your garden and water during cooler parts of the day to reduce evaporation.

Drink Tap Water

Bottled water production uses 6 times more water per liter than tap water. Use a reusable bottle with filtered tap water.
